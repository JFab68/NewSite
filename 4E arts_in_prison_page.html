Transformative Arts Programming

We champion the transformative power of artistic expression in rehabilitation and healing. Our support includes programs like Dr. Susan Bendix's therapeutic dance and motion work at Perryville Prison, which has demonstrated success in promoting personal growth and emotional wellbeing among incarcerated women.

This program recognizes that arts engagement offers unique benefits for incarcerated individuals, including improved emotional regulation, increased self-awareness, enhanced communication skills, and opportunities for positive self-expression. Through dance, visual arts, creative writing, and other modalities, participants develop new perspectives and capabilities that support their rehabilitation.

Evidence-Based Impact

By highlighting successful programs like Dr. Bendix's Think Motion, we demonstrate the value of artistic engagement as a component of effective rehabilitation and personal transformation, contributing to better outcomes both during incarceration and after release.

Spotlight: Think Motion at Perryville

Dr. Susan Bendix's Revolutionary Program

Dr. Susan Bendix's Think Motion program at Perryville Prison represents a groundbreaking approach to therapeutic intervention through dance and movement. This innovative program has demonstrated remarkable success in helping incarcerated women process trauma, develop emotional regulation skills, and build supportive community connections.

The program integrates evidence-based dance/movement therapy techniques with trauma-informed care principles, creating a safe space for participants to explore their experiences, develop coping strategies, and build resilience. Through guided movement exercises, participants learn to reconnect with their bodies in healthy ways and develop tools for managing stress and emotional challenges.

Participants report significant improvements in self-esteem, emotional regulation, and interpersonal relationships. The program has also contributed to reduced disciplinary incidents and improved mental health outcomes among participants.
